About Us

The public grievance system serves as a platform for citizens to report their problems to relevant government departments and receive solutions. The system enables citizens to track the progress of their complaints and escalate them to higher authorities if needed. The public grievance system provides a voice to citizens, promotes transparency, helps resolve issues, and builds trust between citizens and the government.

One of the major benefits of the public grievance system is that it ensures accountability. When citizens have a grievance or complaint, they can use the system to raise their concerns with the relevant government department. The government is then held accountable for addressing and resolving the issue. The public grievance system promotes transparency and accountability in governance as citizens can access information about the status of their complaints and the actions taken by the government to address them.

Another benefit of the public grievance system is that it provides a voice to citizens. Through the system, citizens can voice their concerns and complaints, which enables them to participate in the governance process and hold their government accountable. Citizens who use the system feel empowered and are more likely to engage in their community and participate in the democratic process.

How to Lodge a Complaint

Step 1: Register

Create an account on the Public Grievance Portal to get started.For any queries you can ask question.

Step 2: Submit a Grievance

Once you're registered, you can submit a grievance with just a few clicks.If you dont know to which department your complaint belongs to you can ask question in website.

Step 3: Track Your Grievance

Check the status of your grievance at any time by logging into your account.Moreover when ever your status updated you will be receiving an email.

Step 3: Raise complaint to Higher Authority

If your grievance is not being addressed properly you can raise complaint to higher authority.

Issues that are not considered

  • Personal disputes or issues between individuals that do not involve any public interest or affect the larger community.
  • Matters that are already sub judice or under consideration by a court of law.
  • Complaints that are not specific, clear or supported by evidence, making it difficult to verify or investigate.
  • Issues that are related to private entities or organizations that are not under the purview of the government or public authorities.
  • Matters that are trivial or frivolous in nature, and do not warrant any formal action or intervention.
